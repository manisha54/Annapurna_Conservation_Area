From the subtropical sal forest to perennial snow, this area is home to 22 different forests with 1226 plant species. Among them, 55 species of plants are endemic, only found in Nepal. Annapurna Conservation Area is a habitat to about 101 species of mammals including endangered species like snow leopard, musk deer, Tibetan argali, and Tibetan wolf. This is the only area where all 6 Himalayan Pheasants of Nepal are found.
