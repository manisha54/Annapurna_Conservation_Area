Annapurna Conservation Area

The Annapurna Conservation Area, the largest conservation area in Nepal, was established in 2049 B.S. (1992 A.D.). Annapurna Conservation Area Project (ACAP) was initiated by King Mahendra Trust of Nature Conservation, a non-government organization funded by various international trusts. The management of this area has been done by the collaborative efforts of the National Trust for Nature Conservation (NTNC) and the local population.

This area is developed as one of the major trekking destinations in the country. The increase in revenue earnings and the employment opportunities in this area as made great contributions to uplifting the living standards of the local community. There is high participation of the locals in the conservation activities conducted in this area. In the latest days, the development of transportation, as well as the increasing demand for hydroelectricity, has been causing some difficulties in conservation.

Our Mission

The mission of the Annapurna Conservation Area is to preserve and protect the rich biodiversity and natural resources of the region. Through sustainable conservation practices, community engagement, and responsible tourism, our aim is to ensure the ecological integrity of the area while enhancing the well-being of local communities. We strive to strike a balance between environmental conservation and the socio-economic development of the region for the benefit of present and future generations.

Our Vision

The vision of the Annapurna Conservation Area is to become a global model for sustainable conservation and eco-friendly tourism. We aspire to create an environment where the delicate balance between nature and human activities thrives harmoniously. Through continuous efforts, we envision a future where the Annapurna region remains a pristine and thriving ecosystem, ensuring the well-being of both the natural world and the local communities.
