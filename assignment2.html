Annapurna Conservation Area

The Annapurna Conservation Area, the largest conservation area in Nepal, was established in 2049 B.S. (1992 A.D.). Annapurna Conservation Area Project (ACAP) was initiated by King Mahendra Trust of Nature Conservation, a non-government organization funded by various international trusts. The management of this area has been done by the collaborative efforts of the National Trust for Nature Conservation (NTNC) and the local population.

This area is developed as one of the major trekking destinations in the country. The increase in revenue earnings and the employment opportunities in this area as made great contributions to uplifting the living standards of the local community. There is high participation of the locals in the conservation activities conducted in this area. In the latest days, the development of transportation, as well as the increasing demand for hydroelectricity, has been causing some difficulties in conservation.
